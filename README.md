# Project RUN

Project RUN is a living archive of creative practice around personal computers, video games, electronic media, and the people who used them.

The project begins with a personal collection, but its purpose is larger than ownership. It documents what these objects enabled people to make, learn, play, publish, and share.

## Why “RUN”?

`RUN` is the moment when preparation becomes execution. It is also an immediate reference to home-computer culture: after entering or loading a program, you typed `RUN` and something happened.

## Work areas

1. **The Collection** — inventory and preserve physical objects and their histories.
2. **The Archive** — connect objects to documents, scans, software, recordings, and external collections.
3. **The Workbench** — make the collection usable through restoration, experimentation, interpretation, and new creative work.

See [docs/work-areas.md](docs/work-areas.md) for the fuller description.

## Repository map

- `docs/` — principles, plans, methods, and project decisions
- `collections/` — catalog records for physical and digital materials
- `schemas/` — machine-readable definitions for catalog records
- `research/` — notes and source trails for future investigation
- `exhibits/` — outlines and supporting material for public stories or presentations
- `tools/` — future scripts for validation, indexing, import, and publishing

## Current status

This repository is in its foundation stage. Documents marked **Stub** describe agreed directions that still need research or implementation. Catalog records may include `verification_status: provisional` until details have been checked against the physical item or a reliable source.

## First priorities

- establish a consistent but forgiving metadata model;
- catalog representative items rather than waiting for a perfect system;
- connect physical holdings to trustworthy digital surrogates such as Internet Archive scans;
- preserve provenance, condition, and personal stories alongside technical facts;
- keep the underlying data portable, searchable, and reusable.
